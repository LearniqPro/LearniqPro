Course Overview

Course Title: Computer Fundamentals

Computer Fundamentals is a foundational course for BCA students, designed to introduce them to the basic concepts and principles of computer science. This course provides an essential understanding of computers, their components, and their role in modern society.

Course Objectives:
  1. To familiarize students with the fundamental components of a computer system.
  2. To introduce students to the basic principles of computer operation and architecture.
  3. To develop a basic understanding of computer software and operating systems.
  4. To cultivate essential digital literacy and computer skills.
Assessment Methods:

Assessment methods for this course may include quizzes, assignments, a mid-term exam, and a final exam. Additionally, students might be required to demonstrate their understanding through practical exercises, such as computer lab assignments and hands-on tasks related to computer hardware and software.

Please note that this overview provides a general outline of the course content and assessment methods. The specific topics covered and the evaluation criteria may vary depending on your institution's curriculum and the instructor's preferences. For detailed information about the course syllabus and materials, please refer to the documents provided by your instructor.
